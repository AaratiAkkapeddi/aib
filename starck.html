How can we support our weight with a minimum of material and energy?

In a career stretching over forty years, renowned creator Philippe Starck is responsible for many iconic designs, including some of the most reproduced and celebrated chairs of our time.
But how does a designer with a reputation for challenging expectations continue to push against predictability?

My organic brain cannot imagine what an artificial brain can do...

To get out of his comfort zone, Philippe Starck partnered with manufacturer Kartell to approach a familiar problem with an unfamiliar tool.

He would create a chair with the least amount of material possible, using AI as his design collaborator.

Unlike humans, an AI doesn’t see a chair as a place to sit. It only sees a problem to solve.

For Starck, it’s an exciting opportunity for a fresh perspective on what a chair can be.

A chair that addresses our collective need to do
more with less.
Starck defines the problems for the AI to solve.
How many places must a chair contact your body to effectively support it?
How much weight must it support?
What is the maximum volume of space a chair would ever take up?

Slide to rotate model

Here we see how the AI moves and removes material while respecting the guidelines established by the designer.

The parts of the chair that appear blue represent areas of low stress. Material can be safely removed in these areas without impacting performance.
The AI can generate as many design options as Starck needs, many more than anyone could think of on their own. Early on, the most promising designs are singled out and given small adjustments to steer the results toward better outcomes.
As the software learns Starck’s preferences, it picks up on small details that most people would miss - the slope of the chair leg, simpler forms over complex shapes. Back and forth, they engage in a design dialog.
Stress simulations are run on the final model. The results not only indicate the safety
and durability of the chair, but also ensure that needless material hadn’t been added.

Swipe to spin model

Starck and his design team take the raw results generated by the AI.

They adjust the forms to meet proper ergonomics and apply Starck’s signature curves.

After putting their finishing touches on the proposed design, the next step is to prepare it for manufacturing.

Kartell explores fabrication options with Starck’s design team.

In the spirit of doing more with less, the team at Kartell devise a way to produce the chair using their own byproduct manufacturing material.

It’s the first time in Kartell’s 70-year history that a product is made from 100% recycled plastics.
If a chair design is a reflection of its place in history, what does this chair say about us?
A chair that is digitally optimized for strength, using the least amount of material necessary. A chair made possible with AI, but impossible to produce without the guidance and collaboration of human designers.
More than just a proof of concept, Starck’s AI chair was launched in 2019 during the Salone del Mobile in Milan. It’s now available in Kartell stores worldwide.
